## 3. Definitions

As used in this Privacy Policy:

- **Data Principal:** You -- the individual whose personal data is being processed (patient, caregiver, or clinician)
- **Data Fiduciary:** AIIMS Bhopal -- the entity that determines the purpose and means of data processing
- **Data Processor:** Any entity processing data on behalf of the Data Fiduciary (e.g., AWS for infrastructure hosting)
- **Personal Data:** Any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data
- **Health Data:** Personal data relating to the physical or mental health of a Data Principal, including symptom logs, medication records, pain assessments, and clinical notes
- **Consent:** Free, specific, informed, unconditional, and unambiguous indication of the Data Principal's wishes

## 5. Purpose of Data Collection

We collect and process your personal data for the following specific purposes:

1. **Clinical Care Delivery:** Enabling symptom monitoring, medication management, and communication between patients, caregivers, and clinicians within the AIIMS Bhopal palliative care program
2. **Symptom Monitoring:** Tracking symptom trends over time to support clinical decision-making and timely interventions
3. **Medication Management:** Maintaining medication records, adherence tracking, MEDD calculations, and opioid safety alerts
4. **Clinical Alerts:** Generating notifications to clinicians when symptom scores exceed safety thresholds
5. **Educational Content Delivery:** Providing personalized health education, breathing exercises, and wellness content based on clinical context
6. **Clinical Research (Anonymized):** Contributing anonymized, de-identified data to palliative care research, subject to separate explicit research consent
7. **App Improvement:** Using anonymized usage analytics to improve the application's usability and clinical utility
8. **Communication:** Sending appointment reminders, medication reminders, and clinical notifications
9. **Safety Monitoring:** Detecting crisis indicators (e.g., self-harm risk patterns) for referral to appropriate support services
10. **Regulatory Compliance:** Maintaining records as required by the Clinical Establishments Act, NDPS Act, and other applicable laws

## 7. Consent

### 7.1 How We Obtain Consent

- **Clear and Specific:** Consent is requested for each distinct purpose at the time of data collection
- **Informed:** A clear notice describing the purpose and scope of data processing is displayed before consent is requested
- **Granular:** Separate consent toggles are provided for:
  - Clinical data collection (symptom logs, medications)
  - Push notifications
  - Usage analytics
  - Research participation
  - Legacy message storage
- **Bilingual:** All consent notices are available in both Hindi and English
- **Not Bundled:** Consent for clinical care is not conditional on consent for analytics or research

### 7.2 Consent Records

All consent actions (grants, withdrawals, modifications) are recorded with:
- Timestamp (UTC)
- Consent type and scope
- Method of consent (in-app toggle, verbal witnessed consent)
- Version of privacy policy at time of consent

### 7.3 Impaired Cognition Provisions

For patients with cognitive impairment who cannot provide standard written consent:
- **Witnessed verbal consent** is supported as an alternative
- Witness name, role, and relationship to patient are recorded
- Consent language is provided at a Grade 6 reading level
- Periodic re-validation of consent understanding is prompted

## 8. Data Retention

| Data Category | Retention Period | Legal Basis | Post-Retention Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Clinical data (symptom logs, assessments, medications, clinical notes) | 3 years from last clinical interaction | Clinical Establishments (Registration and Regulation) Act, 2010 | Anonymized for research or securely deleted per patient preference |
| Authentication logs (login attempts, OTP records) | 1 year | IT Act, 2000 -- security incident investigation | Permanently deleted |
| Usage analytics | 2 years | Legitimate purpose -- app improvement | Retained only in anonymized, aggregated form |
| Account profile data | Duration of active account + 3 years | Clinical Establishments Act | Deleted upon verified erasure request (post-retention period) |
| Consent records | 5 years from consent action | DPDPA 2023 compliance evidence | Archived, then deleted |
| Legacy messages | Until retrieved by designated recipient or 5 years post patient's passing, whichever is earlier | Patient consent | Permanently deleted (end-to-end encrypted, unrecoverable after deletion) |
| Data deletion request records | 3 years from completion | DPDPA compliance audit trail | Permanently deleted |
| Crash reports | 2 years | App stability analysis | Always anonymized at collection; deleted after retention |

Where clinical data retention is required by law (Clinical Establishments Act), deletion requests will result in the data being restricted (not actively processed) rather than erased until the retention period expires.

## 9. Data Security

We implement the following technical and organizational measures to protect your data:

### 9.1 Technical Measures

| Measure | Implementation |
|---|---|
| Encryption at rest | AES-256 encryption for all stored personal data (database, file storage) |
| Encryption in transit | TLS 1.3 for all network communication between app, API, and dashboard |
| Authentication | JWT-based token authentication with refresh token rotation |
| Password hashing | bcrypt with cost factor 12 for clinician passwords |
| Local device storage | flutter_secure_storage (Keychain on iOS, EncryptedSharedPreferences on Android) for tokens and sensitive local data |
| Legacy message encryption | End-to-end encryption -- server cannot decrypt content |
| API security | Rate limiting (NestJS Throttler), input validation (class-validator), CORS restrictions |
| HTTP security headers | Helmet.js (CSP, HSTS, X-Frame-Options, etc.) |
| Database security | PostgreSQL row-level security, parameterized queries (SQL injection prevention) |
| Session management | Redis-backed session store with configurable TTL |

### 9.2 Organizational Measures

- Role-based access control (RBAC): Patients, caregivers, clinicians, and administrators have distinct permission sets
- Clinician access requires AIIMS Bhopal institutional credentials
- Audit logging of all data access and modifications
- Regular security assessments and vulnerability scanning
- Staff data protection training
- Incident response plan with defined escalation pathways

## 12. Your Rights as a Data Principal

Under the DPDPA 2023, you have the following rights:

### 12.1 Right to Access (Section 11)

You have the right to:
- Obtain confirmation of whether your personal data is being processed
- Access a summary of your personal data and the processing activities
- Know the identities of all Data Processors and third parties with whom your data has been shared
- View your complete symptom history, medication records, and assessment scores

### 12.2 Right to Correction (Section 11)

You have the right to:
- Correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data
- Update your profile information (name, phone, language preference)
- Request clinician review and correction of clinical data entries

### 12.3 Right to Erasure (Section 12)

You have the right to:
- Request deletion of your personal data
- Receive confirmation of completed deletion
- Note: Clinical data subject to statutory retention (Clinical Establishments Act, 3 years) will be restricted rather than deleted until the retention period expires, after which it will be permanently erased

### 12.4 Right to Data Portability (Section 18)

You have the right to:
- Export your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON or CSV)
- Receive your data for transfer to another service provider
- Export includes: symptom logs, medication records, assessment scores, profile data

### 12.5 Right to Restrict Processing

You have the right to:
- Pause specific types of data processing while maintaining your account
- Disable analytics collection independently of clinical features
- Pause push notifications without affecting data access

### 12.6 Right to Lodge a Complaint (Section 17)

You have the right to:
- Lodge a complaint with the **Data Protection Board of India** if you believe your data rights have been violated
- Contact the DPO first for resolution before approaching the Board

### 12.7 Right to Nominate (Section 13)

You have the right to:
- Nominate a person to exercise your data rights in the event of your death or incapacity
- Update or revoke your nominee at any time
- In PalliCare, the linked caregiver may serve as nominee with appropriate permissions

## 15. Consent Withdrawal

You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time. Consent withdrawal:

### 15.1 How to Withdraw

- **In-App:** Settings > Privacy > Consent Management > toggle off specific consent categories
- **Email:** Send withdrawal request to dpo@pallicare.in
- **Written Request:** Submit to DPO at postal address

### 15.2 Effect of Withdrawal

- Withdrawal is prospective -- it does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal
- Withdrawing clinical data consent will:
  - Stop new symptom log collection
  - Preserve existing data for the statutory retention period (Clinical Establishments Act)
  - Restrict access to stored data (no active processing)
- Withdrawing analytics consent stops all usage tracking immediately
- Withdrawing notification consent stops all push notifications
- Withdrawing research consent removes your data from future research datasets (previously anonymized data cannot be recalled)

### 15.3 Account Implications

- You may withdraw specific consents while keeping your account active
- Withdrawing all consents will effectively deactivate your account
- You may reactivate consent at any time through the same mechanisms

## 16. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, AIIMS Bhopal will:

### 16.1 Notification to Data Protection Board

- Notify the **Data Protection Board of India** within **72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach, per DPDPA 2023 Section 8(6)
- Notification includes: nature of breach, categories and approximate number of affected Data Principals, likely consequences, and remedial measures taken

### 16.2 Notification to Affected Data Principals

- Notify affected individuals **without undue delay** after determining the scope of the breach
- Notification method: In-app notification, SMS to registered phone number, and email (if available)
- Notification includes: nature of the breach, potential impact, steps taken to mitigate, and recommended protective actions

### 16.3 Breach Response

- Immediate containment and assessment
- Forensic investigation to determine scope and root cause
- Remediation of the vulnerability
- Documentation in the breach register
- Post-incident review and security improvement

## 18. Caregiver and Nominee Provisions

### 18.1 Caregiver Access

- Caregivers linked to a patient account can view (but not modify) patient symptom logs and medication records
- Caregiver access is granted by the patient through explicit consent
- Patients can revoke caregiver access at any time
- Caregivers have their own separate account with their own data rights

### 18.2 Post-Mortem Data Access

- Upon a patient's passing, caregiver access to patient data is governed by:
  - The patient's prior consent and nominee designation
  - Applicable laws regarding medical records of deceased persons
  - Legacy message access follows patient-designated recipient rules
- Clinical data may be retained for the statutory period regardless of patient's death

### 18.3 Nominee Rights

- A nominated person may exercise the Data Principal's rights (access, correction, erasure) on their behalf in the event of death or incapacity
- Nominee designation is recorded in-app with timestamp and witness (if applicable)

## 19. Research Data

### 19.1 Separate Consent

- Research use of your data requires **separate, explicit consent** beyond clinical consent
- Research consent is entirely optional and does not affect your access to clinical features
- You may withdraw research consent at any time

### 19.2 Anonymization

- All data used for research is **anonymized before analysis** -- no identifiable information leaves the clinical context
- Anonymization follows ICMR 2017 National Ethical Guidelines
- Anonymized data cannot be re-identified or linked back to individual patients

### 19.3 Ethics Oversight

- All research activities are subject to the AIIMS Bhopal Institutional Ethics Committee (IEC) approval
- Research protocols follow ICMR 2017 National Ethical Guidelines for Biomedical and Health Research
