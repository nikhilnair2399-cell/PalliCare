## 1. Introduction

You are being invited to participate in a research study evaluating a mobile phone application called "PalliCare." This app is designed to help patients manage pain, track symptoms, and communicate with their palliative care team. Before you decide to participate, please read this form carefully and ask any questions you may have.

## 2. Purpose of the Study

The purpose of this study is to evaluate whether the PalliCare mobile app is:
- Easy to use for patients, caregivers, and clinicians
- Helpful in tracking and managing pain and other symptoms
- Useful in improving communication with your care team
- Technically reliable and safe

## 3. What Will Be Asked of You

If you agree to participate, you will be asked to:

1. **Install the PalliCare app** on your Android smartphone (or your caregiver's phone)
2. **Complete a 30-minute training session** on how to use the app
3. **Log your symptoms** (pain, mood, sleep, etc.) at least 3 times per week for 8 weeks
4. **Log your medication intake** daily using the medication tracker
5. **Complete a usability survey** at the end of the study (10 minutes)
6. **Optionally participate** in a brief interview about your experience (20 minutes)

The total study duration is approximately 8 weeks of active app use.

## 4. Risks and Discomforts

The risks associated with this study are minimal:
- **Time commitment**: Using the app will require 5-10 minutes per day
- **Emotional**: Tracking symptoms may occasionally remind you of your illness
- **Privacy**: Your health data will be stored on your phone and a secure server (see Section 7)
- **Technical**: The app may occasionally have bugs or require updates

**Important**: The PalliCare app is NOT a replacement for your medical care. Always contact your doctor for medical emergencies. The emergency number 112 is available in the app.

## 5. Benefits

Potential benefits of participating include:
- Better tracking and awareness of your pain patterns
- Improved communication of symptoms to your care team
- Medication reminders to help with adherence
- Access to educational content about pain management
- Contributing to research that may help future palliative care patients

## 6. Voluntariness

Your participation in this study is entirely voluntary. You may:
- Refuse to participate without any penalty
- Withdraw from the study at any time without affecting your medical care
- Request deletion of all your data at any time
- Skip any survey question you do not wish to answer

Your decision will NOT affect the quality of care you receive at AIIMS Bhopal.

## 7. Confidentiality and Data Protection

In accordance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDPA) 2023:

- Your personal data (name, phone number) will be kept strictly confidential
- Health data (symptom logs, medication records) will be encrypted and stored securely
- Only the research team will have access to identifiable data
- Published results will use anonymized, aggregate data only
- You have the right to access, correct, and delete your personal data
- Data will be retained for 1 year after study completion, then permanently deleted
- No data will be shared with any third party

## 8. Compensation

There is no direct monetary compensation for participating. A token travel reimbursement of INR 200 per visit may be provided for study-related visits beyond routine clinical care.
